The Map · Behind the data

A census, not a newsfeed

We hold three monthly snapshots of Bình Thạnh from Overture Maps — April, May, June 2026. Put side by side, they answer a tempting question: how many shops opened here this spring? The honest answer is that this data cannot tell you — and seeing why is the whole point.

This page compares Overture only against itself, release to release. No external ground truth — so it measures the data's own behaviour, not the neighbourhood's.

The 1,167 that weren't changes

Between May and June, Overture reported zero new places and 1,167 "recategorized" — which looks like upheaval. It isn't. Overture's June schema (v1.17.0) renamed its category vocabulary. And the streets stood still

The physical layers barely move over a quarter — expected for a built-out inner district. Overture is refreshing its map, not watching the district change.

The takeaway. Across three months, Overture's Bình Thạnh holds steady at about 25,300 places — a net drift of just +101. The thousands of IDs churning underneath are the source recycling identifiers, and the biggest apparent "change" was a rename. Overture is a dependable monthly census of what stands here. It is not a feed of what opened this week — and the data itself, compared only to its own past, is what proves it.
