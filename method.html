How this was built

Data provenance, the precise clip, and the honest caveats.

From OSM full history to a yearly snapshot set

The story draws on the OpenStreetMap full-history extract for Vietnam, loaded into a self-hosted PostGIS database. For each year 2015–2025 we render a point-in-time snapshot (1 January) into its own schema, then measure each schema identically. Eleven schemas, one per year, give the decade as an annual series.

The precise clip (decision #1)

Every figure is clipped to the historical Quận Bình Thạnh polygon with a true geometric intersection (ST_Intersection), not a bounding-box overlap. The bbox shortcut over-counts edge features by 60–74%; the precise clip is what makes the 963 → 5,268 road-way figures trustworthy.

The cross-database boundary bridge (decision #2)

The district polygon lives in the current-OSM database; the yearly history lives in another. We pull the boundary as WKT from the current database and pass it as a bound parameter into each history query — no cross-database federation, just one geometry carried across.

An abolished district

Quận Bình Thạnh was dissolved in Hồ Chí Minh City's mid-2025 administrative reform. Its OSM relation (-3797166) is now untagged but still carries a valid geometry, so we fetch it by id. Every snapshot is clipped to the district as it existed from 2015 to mid-2025 — a fixed footprint across the whole decade.

What the numbers do and don't say

Points of interest are counted as OSM nodes only; places mapped as building polygons are not included, so amenity and shop counts are conservative. Café genuinely overtook fuel under this clip (café 6 → 62, fuel flat at 24), but cafés are not the clipped 2025 number-one amenity — restaurants are. We keep the page truthful to the clipped figures rather than to a coarser earlier probe.

Source: OpenStreetMap full history, self-hosted PostGIS. Built with osmkit.
