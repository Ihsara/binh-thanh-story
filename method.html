How we know

The boundary

Every feature is clipped to the historical district polygon (OSM relation -3797166) before counting, so the numbers describe a fixed area through the decade.

The 2018→19 “mapping leap” is mapping, not building

Road ways tripled in one year because a community mapping effort traced the existing alley mesh — the alleys were already there; OpenStreetMap caught up. This is a story about a place becoming legible, not about new construction.

The 2023 shop dip is a data artifact

Mapped shops fall ~253 → 173 around 2023. Shops did not close — a retagging/import change in OSM moved some features between shop=* and other tags. Treat the shop curve's late wobble as noise, not neighbourhood decline.

Café overtook fuel — but café is not #1

Under the precise district clip, cafés climbed from rank 8 to rank 2 (count 6 → 62) while fuel stayed flat (~24). Café genuinely overtook fuel. But the single most-mapped amenity in 2025 is restaurants (77), not cafés. An earlier coarse whole-snapshot probe suggested “café is #1”; the precise clip tells the truer, smaller story.
