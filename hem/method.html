How this was made — and what it cannot say

Method & honesty

This story reads address depth straight out of OpenStreetMap street names for Quận Bình Thạnh. A road's depth is the number of slashes in its hẻm path: a named boulevard is depth −1, Hẻm 22 X is depth 0, Hẻm 22/5 X is depth 1, and so on. The parser reaches the deepest observed fork at depth 3.

Parser coverage: 100% of the 2,268 hẻm names in the district parse cleanly into a depth, a path, and a parent arterial.

Three things this data cannot tell you

Deep hẻm is under-mapped. The further an alley sits from a boulevard, the less likely OpenStreetMap has it — and the less likely its shops and clinics are tagged. Every share and count here is a share of what is mapped, so the deepest levels (d2, d3) rest on very few points. Read them as direction, not magnitude.
OSM time is mapping time, not building time. When a year shows a jump in alleys, that is when volunteers drew them, not when the city built them. The 2019 surge is a mapping campaign filling in alleys that already existed — the hẻm did not appear in 2019, the map did.
The yearly snapshots see only node POIs. The play-button frames come from per-year history snapshots that carry point (node) amenities only; a shop mapped as a building footprint without a centre node will not appear. The present-day map is richer than any single past frame.
